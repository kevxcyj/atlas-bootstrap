The Kamen Rider Series is a Japanese superhero tokusatsu franchise created by manga artist Shotaro Ishinomori, which began in 1971 with the original Kamen Rider television series. Each series generally features a motorcycle-riding superhero with a varying motif who fights supervillains. Over the years, the franchise has created television and theatrical sequels with its popularity expanding the franchise into other media such as manga, anime, novels, games, stage plays, and audio dramas. Each Kamen Rider is equiped with a Driver, which is the belt that allows them to transform. This has been a staple throughout the series, taking many different iterations. The transformation slogan "Henshin" coined by the original Kamen Rider, is still used in the current series today. While Kamen Rider is a hero of justice, many of the heros only seek revenge, which inevitaly leads to justice in their own ways.

The Showa Kamen Rider Series is the first of three production eras of the Kamen Rider Series. It refers to the Showa period ("period of enlightened peace/harmony"), also known as the Showa era, the period of Japanese history corresponding to the reign of the Sh≈çwa Emperor, Hirohito, from December 25, 1926 through January 7, 1989, and the period in which the majority of the Showa Rider productions occured in. The Kamen Rider Series began in the middle of the Showa-era, aboout 28 years after the end of World War II and the start of what is known as Post-War Japan. The Showa-era ended in 1989 during the run of Kamen Rider Black RX with the death of Emperor Hirihito.
Being completely absent from air during the 1990s, the franchise was kept maily alive by stage shows, CDs and special movies, though fans classify these movies as apart of the Showa era to treat Shotarto Ishiminori's passing in 1998 as the true ending to the Showa era.

The Heisei Kamen Rider Series is the second production era of the Kamen Rider Series. It refers to Heisei, also known as the Heisei era, the period of Japanese history corresponding to the reign of Heisei Emperor Akihito, from January 8, 1989, the first day after the death of the previous Emperor, to his abdication on April 30th, 2019. In accordance with Japanese customs, Akihito will be renamed "Emperor Heisei", upon his future death.
After its absence in the 1990s, the series was truly revived in the Heisei Era, starting with Kamen Rider Kuuga in 2000. During the summer of 1999, Kuuga became publicized through magazine ads and commercials and on January 30, 2000, Kamen Rider Kuuga premiered to high ratings.
Series after Decade, starting with Kamen Rider W and ending with Kamen Rider Zi-O , is what fans recognize as the Heisei Era Phase 2. Toei unofficially recognizes this division of the era, since its series have a different airing period, and that its series contains the greatest amount of new trends.

Shotaro Ishinomori

Shotaro Ishinomori (born Shotaro Onodera) was a Japanese manga artist who served as an influential figure in manga, anime, and tokusatsu. Onodera grew up in the town of Ishinomori in Tome, Miyagi Prefecture, where his interests in manga surfaced in middle school. After starting his own group known as Bokuju Itteki, named after the book by Shiki Nasaoka, he entered and won several manga competitions, where he would catch the attention of Osamu Tezuka and become his assistant. In 1963, Ishinomori created Cyborg 009, which became the first super-powered hero team created in Japan. In 1971, he worked with Toei Company to produce Kamen Rider which led to the birth of transforming superheroes who transform by doing a pose, and use martial arts to fight henchmen and monsters. This resulted in many sequel shows to this day including Himitsu Sentai Gorenger, The first Super Sentai series, which is the source material for the American show Power Rangers.
